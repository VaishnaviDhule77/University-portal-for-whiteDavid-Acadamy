What is BCom?

Understanding the Foundation of Commerce Education

Bachelor of Commerce (BCom)

BCom (Bachelor of Commerce) is a 3-year undergraduate degree program that focuses on commerce, finance, accounting, and business management. It is one of the most popular courses in India, providing students with a strong foundation in business principles and practices. The program equips students with analytical, financial, and managerial skills essential for various career opportunities in the corporate world, banking sector, and entrepreneurship.

Duration

  • 3 Years (6 Semesters)
  • Full-time Program
  • Semester System
  • Annual Examinations
  • Internship Opportunities
  • Project Work

Objectives

  • Build Strong Foundation
  • Develop Analytical Skills
  • Understanding Business Operations
  • Financial Management
  • Entrepreneurial Development
  • Professional Ethics

Admission Process

Step-by-Step Guide to Secure Your BCom Seat

Important Dates

  • Application Start: May 2024
  • CUET Exam: May 15-31, 2024
  • Result Declaration: June 2024
  • Counseling Start: July 2024
  • Admission Start: August 2024
  • Classes Begin: August 2024

Application Steps

  • Register on official portal
  • Fill application form
  • Upload documents
  • Pay application fee
  • Download admit card
  • Appear for entrance exam
  • Check result
  • Attend counseling

Fee Structure

  • Application Fee: ₹500-1000
  • Tuition Fee: ₹20,000-1,00,000/year
  • Hostel Fee: ₹60,000-1,20,000/year
  • Exam Fee: ₹2000-5000/semester
  • Library Fee: ₹2000-5000/year
  • Lab Fee: ₹3000-8000/year
  • Other Fees: ₹5000-10,000/year

Complete Admission Timeline

  • Phase 1 (Jan-Mar): Research colleges and check eligibility
  • Phase 2 (Apr-May): Prepare for entrance exams, fill applications
  • Phase 3 (May-Jun): Appear for exams, await results
  • Phase 4 (Jul-Aug): Counseling, seat allocation, document verification
  • Phase 5 (Aug): Fee payment, admission confirmation

Important Notes:

  • Apply to multiple colleges for better options
  • Keep original documents ready for verification
  • Pay fees on time to confirm admission
  • Check for scholarship opportunities
  • Stay updated with college websites
  • Prepare for personal interviews (if required)
