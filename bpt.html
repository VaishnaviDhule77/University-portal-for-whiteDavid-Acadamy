Course Overview

Everything you need to know about BPT program

Program Duration

4.5 years including 6 months of mandatory internship. Comprehensive training in physiotherapy techniques, patient care, and clinical practice.

Degree Type

Undergraduate professional degree. Recognized by Indian Association of Physiotherapists (IAP) and approved by PCI.

Core Focus

Physical rehabilitation, therapeutic exercises, manual therapy, electrotherapy, and patient assessment & management.

Clinical Training

Extensive hands-on training in hospitals, rehabilitation centers, sports clinics, and specialty physiotherapy departments.

Eligibility Criteria

Requirements for BPT admission

Academic Requirements

  • 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum 50% marks in PCB (45% for reserved categories)
  • English as a compulsory subject
  • Age limit: 17-25 years as on admission date
  • Valid score in NEET or state-level entrance exam
  • Medical fitness certificate required

Recommended Skills

  • Strong interpersonal and communication skills
  • Empathy and patience for patient care
  • Good physical stamina and fitness
  • Scientific temperament and analytical thinking
  • Teamwork and collaboration abilities
  • Continuous learning mindset

Frequently Asked Questions

Common queries about BPT program

What is the duration of BPT course?

BPT is a 4.5-year undergraduate program including 6 months of compulsory rotatory internship. The course provides comprehensive training in physiotherapy theory and practical clinical skills.

Is NEET mandatory for BPT admission?

Yes, NEET-UG is mandatory for admission to most government and reputed private colleges. However, some private universities conduct their own entrance tests or offer management quota seats.

Can I pursue MPT after BPT?

Yes, after completing BPT and 6 months internship, you can pursue MPT (Master of Physiotherapy) in various specializations like orthopedics, neurology, sports, cardiopulmonary, etc.

Is BPT a doctorate degree?

BPT is an undergraduate professional degree. BPT graduates can use the prefix 'Dr.' and are recognized as medical professionals. For higher qualifications, one can pursue MPT and Ph.D.
